Seminar Info

This seminar is designed to promote the research of Korean scholars by sharing cutting‑edge studies, fostering networking opportunities, and supporting PhD students in their academic and professional journeys. The initiative was proposed by Professors Young‑Hoon Park and Jiwoong Shin.

Objectives

  • Support PhD Students: Help PhD graduates transition into academia (e.g., job search guidance, and networking opportunities).
  • Bridging Academia and Industry: Provide a platform (from Korea) to connect researchers with industry professionals, fostering partnerships for real‑world data projects and applied research opportunities.
  • Insights from Senior Scholars: Offer tips and strategies from established scholars to navigate publication and career advancement.
  • Thematic Lectures: Explore lectures on specific themes by renowned scholars.

Format

  • Platform: Each seminar is conducted via Zoom.
  • Structure: 45‑minute talk, followed by a 15‑minute discussion session (discussion is optional).
  • Schedule: Fridays at 11:00 AM (Eastern Time) but may vary depending on speaker availability.
  • Frequency: We plan to host 4–5 talks per semester. Topics and formats are evolving to maximize engagement and value, including opportunities for industry practitioners to join and contribute.
